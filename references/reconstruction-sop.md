# 玄清小红书爆款重构 SOP

Use this reference when producing a more rigorous, repeatable transformation pipeline.

## Pipeline

1. Identify the traffic object.
   - Who is supposed to stop scrolling?
   - What do they want immediately?
   - What are they afraid of losing?

2. Convert the topic into a market-tested angle.
   - From "I want to say" to "the audience cannot ignore".
   - From broad value to one sharp desire.
   - From explanation to conflict, contrast, or shortcut.

3. Choose the dominant traffic mechanism.
   - Fear: mistakes, loss, falling behind, wasted money
   - Greed: shortcuts, templates, resource lists, high ROI
   - Vanity: identity, taste, superiority, insider knowledge
   - Relief: finally understood, less effort, fewer wrong turns
   - Controversy: opinion split, taboo truth, anti-common-sense

4. Build the cover.
   - One main promise or threat.
   - One visual proof or contrast.
   - One identity label when relevant.
   - Avoid vague slogans.

5. Build the title set.
   - Produce multiple mechanisms before choosing.
   - Avoid picking the prettiest title; pick the one with the strongest scroll-stop reason.
   - Prefer specificity, numbers, audience labels, and visible stakes.

6. Assemble the body.
   - Hook: stop the reader.
   - Pain: make the reader feel the current cost.
   - Insight: explain the hidden reason.
   - Method: make action feel easy.
   - Proof: show why to trust it.
   - CTA: create a low-friction next action.

7. Add distribution signals.
   - Keywords in first sentence.
   - Search terms in body naturally.
   - Hashtags that combine broad category, niche intent, and identity.
   - Comments designed around real audience disagreement or self-identification.

## Diagnostic Checklist

- Is the audience obvious within 1 second?
- Is the benefit concrete enough to screenshot?
- Does the first sentence create urgency?
- Is there a visible proof asset?
- Does every section serve click, retention, save, comment, or conversion?
- Is the CTA specific enough that a user knows exactly what to comment or do?
- Are claims supported by user-provided facts or clearly marked as placeholders?

## Output Compression

When the user asks for a quick version, output only:

1. Top 5 titles
2. Rewritten body
3. Hashtags

When the user asks for a full version, output the full `SKILL.md` output format.
